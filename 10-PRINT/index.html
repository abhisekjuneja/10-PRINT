10 PRINT

10 PRINT CHR$(205.5+RND(1)); : GOTO 10
10 PRINT is a one-line Commodore 64 BASIC Program: 10 PRINT CHR$(205.5+RND(1)); : GOTO 10

You can see the Program perform its magic on an Emulated Version of the Oiginal Commodore 64 in this Video posted by Thomas Winningham

I've created a JavaScript Clone of the Program using P5.js and I've embedded it to this page for you to enjoy.

Nick Montfort, Patsy Baudoin, John Bell, Ian Bogost Jeremy Douglass, Mark C. Marino, Michael Mateas Casey Reas, Mark Sample, and Noah Vawter have collaborated and created a Book on this remarkable piece of code which can be downloaded Here.
This book takes a single line of code—the extremely concise BASIC program for the Commodore 64 inscribed in the title—and uses it as a lens through which to consider the phenomenon of creative computing and the way computer programs exist in culture. The authors of this collaboratively written book treat code not as merely functional but as a text—in the case of 10 PRINT, a text that appeared in many different printed sources—that yields a story about its making, its purpose, its assumptions, and more. They consider randomness and regularity in computing and art, the maze in culture, the popular BASIC programming language, and the highly influential Commodore 64 computer. - The MIT Press Overview
